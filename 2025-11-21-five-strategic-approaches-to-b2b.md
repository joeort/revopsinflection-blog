Territory design isn’t just about drawing lines on a map; it’s a **strategic lever** that dictates market coverage, sales focus, and ultimately, revenue predictability. As a RevOps leader who has scaled B2B SaaS businesses from $10M to $100M, I’ve seen firsthand how a well-designed territory plan can unlock massive growth, while a poorly designed one can stall a company.

The choice of territory model should always align with your **current stage of maturity** and your **go-to-market (GTM) objective**.

* * *

### **The Strategic Imperative: Know Your Terrain (TAM)**

Before assigning a single account, the most powerful strategic lever you have is **identifying your True North: your Total Addressable Market (TAM)**.

It is impossible to design productive territories without first segmenting and scoring your market. The shift from using **account volume** to using **opportunity value** as the balancing metric is the single greatest driver of sales productivity.

> **Why TAM and High-Propensity Accounts are Non-Negotiable:**
> 
> Targeting your **top propensity accounts** (those most likely to buy and succeed) allows you to reallocate AE time away from low-probability efforts. If an AE’s territory is 50% high-propensity accounts versus 10%, their **Pipeline per Account Executive (PPAE)** and **Win Rate** will dramatically increase, shrinking the cost of sales and shortening the path to profitability… a key metric for any leadership or PE review.

Here are the five primary approaches to structuring your sales territories, mapped to your company’s strategic maturity.

### 1\. 🌎 Geo-Based Territories (Stage: Early Scalability)

This is the entry point, defining an Account Executive’s (AE’s) scope by **geographical boundaries**.

-   **Strategic Lever:** **Simple Market Coverage.** It’s easy to administer and ensures no large geographic area is left untouched when you are still validating your ICP.
    
-   **Best For (Maturity):** Companies in the **$10M to $30M ARR range** selling a relatively **horizontal solution**. You need to cover a wide area to find your next best-fit customers.
    
-   **Leadership Insight:** While easy to set up, pure geo-based models are a sign of **GTM immaturity** if you remain there too long. It often leads to **unbalanced territories** based on account count, not actual opportunity value. _Transition out of this model as soon as your ICP is validated._
    

* * *

### 2\. 🎯 Vertical/Industry-Based Territories (Stage: Product Specialization)

Alignment dedicates AEs to specific industries (e.g., Financial Services, Healthcare, Manufacturing).

-   **Strategic Lever:** **Domain Expertise and Narrative Lock-In.** By focusing on an industry, AEs develop deep knowledge, leading to highly relevant conversations, faster qualification, and a stronger competitive narrative.
    
-   **Best For (Maturity):** Companies in the **$30M to $150M ARR range** where industry-specific pain points require a tailored solution or implementation expertise.
    
-   **Leadership Insight:** This is a key step toward **GTM specialization**. It instantly **increases win rates** and **shortens sales cycles** because AEs speak the customer’s language. The focus shifts from **what** your product does to **how** it solves a specific vertical problem. This signals a sophisticated approach to the market. Be aware that for every vertical you create/target, you need to come up with marketing resources/content/messaging to support each on independently. It’s not a one-size fits all strategy.
    

* * *

### 3\. 📝 Named Accounts (Stage: Enterprise Focus & Predictability)

Manually curated lists of high-potential companies assigned to specific, senior AEs.

-   **Strategic Lever:** **Deep, Focused Effort on High-ACV Targets.** It removes market noise and ensures your most skilled, expensive talent is exclusively focused on the largest potential deals.
    
-   **Best For (Maturity):** Companies **$100M+ ARR** where the top 100-500 accounts represent a disproportionate amount of your potential future revenue.
    
-   **Leadership Insight:** This approach is critical for **revenue predictability**. It allows you to build Account-Based Marketing (ABM) motions around specific targets, increasing the quality of pipeline and providing a clear forecast path. From a PE standpoint, a clear **Named Account Strategy** demonstrates a deliberate, controlled path to significant revenue growth.
    

* * *

### 4\. 🔄 Target Accounts with Check-In/Check-Out (Stage: Operational Efficiency)

A dynamic model designed to maintain an optimal, consistent workload of high-propensity accounts for AEs.

-   **Strategic Lever:** **Maximizing AE Time on High-Propensity Targets.** It ensures AEs are always working the highest-scoring accounts and prevents them from wasting time on low-fit prospects, directly boosting productivity metrics.
    
-   **Best For (Maturity):** Higher velocity sales teams in the **$50M to $300M ARR range** that rely heavily on outbound sales and need to maintain a fixed, high-quality opportunity capacity per AE. If you’re selling 1-2 deals/mo, this is likely where you should land.
    
-   **Leadership Insight:** This is the most **data-intensive and technically challenging** model but is crucial for maximizing productivity. It requires robust RevOps infrastructure to manage the central pool, apply advanced **predictive scoring**, and automate assignment. The data from “checked-out” accounts is a feedback loop to refine your **ICP and marketing spend**.
    

* * *

### 5\. 👑 Strategic Accounts (The Reserved Top Tier)

The largest, most complex, or highly-leveraged accounts **explicitly removed from all standard territories.**

-   **Strategic Lever:** **Protecting & Growing Anchor Revenue.** This ring-fences your most valuable, low-churn, high-LTV customers, ensuring they receive executive-level attention (often through dedicated Account Management/CS teams) to maximize Net Retention Rate.
    
-   **Best For (Maturity):** Any company that has an **established, multi-year customer base** where 5-10% of customers drive 50%+ of recurring revenue.
    
-   **Leadership Insight:** These accounts are the bedrock of your valuation (high NRR is a major PE driver). RevOps must ensure they are tracked with a unique set of success metrics and are **excluded from all transactional sales motions** to protect the relationship. This “carve-out” is an insurance policy for your most valuable revenue stream.
    

* * *

### **Conclusion: From Volume to Value**

The trajectory of successful B2B SaaS territory design mirrors the maturity of the company: **moving from simple coverage (Geo) to specialized focus (Vertical/Named) and finally, to pure efficiency (Check-In/Check-Out and Strategic Carve-Outs).**

Your priority as a leader is to demand that your RevOps team moves the conversation from **“How many accounts does the AE have?”** to **“What is the total potential dollar value and propensity score of the accounts they are working?”** That is the strategic lever that directly translates into increased team productivity and a clear, profitable path to $1B.
