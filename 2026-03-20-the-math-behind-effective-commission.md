This is where most CFOs and Board members get the math wrong on quotas/comp.  
  
They look at a $300k commission check and see a "cost" problem. As an operator and PE partner, I see an efficiency win.  
  
Let’s pull back the curtain on the math of the Effective Commission Rate (ECR).  
To a PE firm, the ECR is the ultimate truth-teller of sales efficiency. It is the total cost of the human (Fixed + Variable) divided by the revenue they produced.  
  
1\. The "Fixed Cost Coverage" Principle  
In B2B SaaS, every rep starts the year with a "Negative Contribution." You’ve already spent the money on their base salary, their health insurance, their 401k match, and their $1,200/mo tech stack (SFDC, Gong, Navigator, etc.).  
  
Let’s look at a standard mid-market AE model:  
\-OTE: $200,000 (50/50 split: $100k Base / $100k Commission)  
\-Quota: $1,000,000  
\-Base Commission Rate: 10%  
\-Fully Burdened Fixed Cost: $140,000 (Base + ~40% for taxes, benefits, and tech)

The Insight: Even though you are paying the top performer a higher percentage on their marginal deals, their Effective Commission Rate actually drops. They are "cheaper" per dollar of revenue because their $140k "fixed nut" is being spread across a much larger revenue base.  
  
2\. The "Marginal Profitability" Trap  
When a Board says, "We need more heads to hit the number," they are often choosing to buy more Fixed Costs instead of more Variable Results.  
  
If you hire a new rep to get that extra $500k in revenue instead of letting your top performer hit their accelerators:  
\-New Hire Cost: You pay $140k in fixed costs + $50k in commission=$190k.  
\-Top Performer Accelerator: You pay $75k in accelerated commission=$75k.  
  
By pushing that revenue to the top performer, you just saved the business $115,000 in EBITDA. In a 10x environment, you just added over $1M in Enterprise Value simply by choosing accelerators over headcount.  
  
3\. The "Hidden Tax" of the Ramping Rep  
From a PE standpoint, the biggest risk to a PortCo is Ramp Risk. \* Failure Rate: ~30% of new hires will never hit quota.  
\-Ramp Cost: For the first 6 months, a new hire's ECR is essentially infinite (Cost / $0 Revenue).  
\-Management Drag: Every new hire takes 20% of a Sales Manager’s time.  
A rep in accelerators is a fully ramped, de-risked asset. They require less management, have higher win rates, and higher deal integrity (less discounting).  
  
When I’m advising a CRO, I tell them: "Manage your floor, but feed your ceiling."  
1) Don't Cap Commissions: It’s the fastest way to send your top 5% (who produce 25% of your revenue) to the door.  
2) Aggressive Accelerators: Set them high enough that it’s painful to pay, but realize that "pain" is actually the highest-margin revenue you will ever book.  
3) Decelerators for Underperformers: If a rep is at 50% of quota, their ECR is 38-40%. That is unsustainable. You don't need more reps; you need fewer people who can't cover their own "fixed nut." Paying your bottom reps less allows you to pay your better reps more.
